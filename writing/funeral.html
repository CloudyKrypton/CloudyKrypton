Funeral for Another Day Wasted

Clutching a cluster of carnations from mourning till midnight,
We stand over blackened soil and a dirt-brown coffin,
Our eyes laden with mirth, heart heavy with melancholy,
This routine is becoming incredibly numbing.
I stare at the ground, hoping that it'll come back to life,
And spring out of the ground roaring, "April fools!"
My mother dabs a tear at the corner of her eye,
With a polyester handkerchief.
I cast my flowers into the grave, what a stupid day.
