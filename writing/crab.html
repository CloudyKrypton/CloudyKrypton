grocery store crab

A sight encountered before, but today, with new eyes

Scene: a grocery store, once a backdrop,

Now, my throat drops

At this crustaceous slaughter house.

The living dead. Bodies tossed together,

mocking live burial, the plague that infected them,

a construct - seafood farming.

Jerky movement here and there indicted

They were fresh; (half off if you get five)

Untold suffering; (they were still faintly alive).

Their claws turned up to God,

Who will not respond in turn.

Instead, my dad answers their claws with tongs.

The grocery store crab cannot scream,

And neither can I.
